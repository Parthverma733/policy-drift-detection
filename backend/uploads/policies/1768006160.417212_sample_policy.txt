GOVERNMENT EDUCATION SCHEME POLICY DOCUMENT

Policy Title: Universal Literacy Enhancement Program (ULEP)

OBJECTIVE:
This policy aims to achieve universal literacy across all districts, with special focus on rural areas and districts with literacy rates below 70%. The program mandates minimum coverage of 80% of the target population and requires consistent monthly reporting.

TARGET GROUPS:
1. Rural districts with literacy rate below 70%
2. Urban districts with literacy rate below 70%
3. Marginalized communities including scheduled castes and scheduled tribes
4. Women and children in below poverty line households

QUANTITATIVE CONSTRAINTS:
- Minimum coverage: 85% of target population must be reached
- Maximum monthly variance: 10% month-over-month change is allowed
- Fund utilization: Minimum 90% of allocated funds must be utilized
- Consistency period: Monthly reporting is mandatory

TEMPORAL REQUIREMENTS:
- Reporting frequency: Monthly
- Deadline: Reports must be submitted within 5 days of month end
- Consistency: Implementation must maintain consistency across all reporting periods

RESOURCE ALLOCATION RULES:
- Funds must be distributed equitably across all districts
- Priority allocation to districts with literacy rate < 70%
- No district should receive zero allocation
- Allocation should be proportional to population size

MONITORING AND EVALUATION:
- Monthly review of coverage metrics
- Quarterly assessment of fund utilization
- Annual evaluation of program effectiveness
- Immediate action required for districts with coverage below 80%

IMPLEMENTATION GUIDELINES:
All implementing agencies must ensure:
1. Coverage does not drop below 85% threshold
2. Monthly variance remains within 10% limit
3. Fund utilization meets 90% minimum requirement
4. Regular monthly reporting without gaps
